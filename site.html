About Me

I am Ahmed Safwat, a final-year student in the Mechatronics Engineering Department at the Faculty of Engineering – Banha University, expected to graduate in 2026.

Driven by a growing passion for artificial intelligence, I began developing my skills through several specialized training programs. I started with an Internet of Things (IoT) course at the National Telecommunications Institute (ITI) (30 hours).
Followed by an intensive 40-hour training program in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning by Microsoft in collaboration with Sprints. During this program, I developed a heart disease prediction application, which was published on GitHub, and I received a certificate of successful completion.

I also joined another 120-hour training program with the National Telecommunications Institute (NTI) in Machine Learning and Data Analysis, during which I developed a project to predict whether a newborn would require admission to the neonatal intensive care unit (NICU). This project was also published on GitHub.

I aim to integrate my engineering background with AI technologies to develop innovative, data-driven solutions that address real-world challenges.
